# Common Tax Return Mistakes

Errors frequently found in self-prepared and accountant-prepared returns.

## Filing Status Errors

### 1. Not Evaluating All Filing Options
- Married couples defaulting to MFJ without checking if MFS saves more (rare but possible with medical expenses or student loans)
- NRA visa holders defaulting to 1040-NR without evaluating RA MFJ election
- Single filers who qualify for Head of Household (unmarried with qualifying dependent)

### 2. Wrong Filing Status for NRA Filers
- NRAs cannot file MFJ on 1040-NR — must elect RA under §6013(g) first
- Accountants sometimes file NRA when RA MFJ would save thousands (especially for homeowners)

## Deduction Errors

### 3. Using Outdated SALT Cap
- 2025 SALT cap is $40,000 MFJ / $20,000 MFS (with phase-down for high AGI)
- Some software or accountants may still use the old $10,000 / $5,000 cap

### 4. Missing Deductible Taxes in SALT
Common items left out of the SALT deduction:
- State disability insurance (SDI/CASDI) from W-2 box 19
- Vehicle license fee (VLF) portion of car registration
- Local income taxes
- Property tax paid directly (not through escrow) — especially mid-year purchases

### 5. Mortgage Interest Missed or Miscalculated
- Loan transferred mid-year → two 1098 forms from different servicers (both must be included)
- NRA returns correctly exclude mortgage interest, but the accountant should have evaluated RA election
- Exceeding the $750K loan limit without pro-rating the deduction

### 6. Itemizing When Standard Is Better (or Vice Versa)
- Not comparing totals — especially after SALT cap limits itemized amount
- Forgetting that MFS requires both spouses to use the same method

## Income Errors

### 7. Missing Income Sources
- State tax refund from prior year (taxable if you itemized previously)
- Bank interest, money market dividends
- Crypto/digital asset transactions
- Side income reported on 1099-NEC or 1099-K

### 8. Investment Income Classification (NRA Returns)
- Portfolio dividends and interest are FDAP (flat rate or treaty rate on Schedule NEC)
- Accountants sometimes report all income as ECI at graduated rates — may overtax or undertax
- Treaty-exempt income (e.g., portfolio interest with 1042-S exemption code 05) included as taxable

### 9. Qualified Dividends Not Given Preferential Rate
- Qualified dividends (1099-DIV box 1b) should be taxed at 0%/15%/20%, not ordinary rates
- Must use the Qualified Dividends and Capital Gain Tax Worksheet

## Withholding and Payment Errors

### 10. W-2 Data Entry Errors
- Transposed numbers between box 1 (wages) and box 16 (state wages)
- Medicare wages (box 5) entered incorrectly — especially when FICA-exempt for part of the year
- Missing W-2 from a short-term or mid-year employer

### 11. Missing Withholding Credits
- 1042-S withholding not claimed (line 25g on 1040-NR)
- Estimated tax payments not entered
- Excess Social Security tax credit when working for multiple employers

### 12. Additional Medicare Tax Errors
- Applied to wrong wage amount (should be W-2 box 5, not box 1)
- Wrong threshold used for filing status ($250K MFJ, $200K single, $125K MFS)
- Filed Form 8959 when wages are below threshold

## Credit Errors

### 13. Education Credits Not Claimed or Wrong Credit Chosen
- American Opportunity Credit (up to $2,500, partially refundable) vs Lifetime Learning Credit (up to $2,000)
- Income phase-outs not checked — high earners may be fully phased out
- NRAs cannot claim education credits

### 14. Child Tax Credit Missed
- $2,000 per qualifying child under 17
- Phase-out begins at $200K single / $400K MFJ
- Additional Child Tax Credit (refundable portion) requires earned income

## How to Review a Return

1. Verify all W-2/1099/1098 amounts match the source documents
2. Check filing status is optimal — run alternative scenarios
3. Confirm SALT cap uses current-year rules
4. Verify mortgage interest is included (RA) or correctly excluded (NRA)
5. Check investment income treatment (graduated rates vs flat rates vs treaty)
6. Verify withholding totals match all W-2s, 1099s, and 1042-S forms
7. Confirm prior-year carryforwards (refunds, losses, credits)
8. Check Additional Medicare Tax only applies to actual Medicare wages above threshold
9. Verify education and child credits are claimed if eligible
